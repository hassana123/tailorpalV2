# TailorPal Non-Technical Shop Operations Guide

Generated: March 4, 2026
Audience: Shop owners, managers, and team members (non-technical users)

## 1. What This Guide Is
This is a plain-language guide for running your shop inside TailorPal.

It explains, step by step, how to use your dashboard for:
- customer management
- measurement management
- order management
- catalog management
- inventory management
- staff management
- shop settings and day-to-day workflow

You do not need technical knowledge to use this guide.

## 2. How Your Shop Workspace Is Organized
When you open your shop dashboard, you will see:
- Dashboard: summary of customers, orders, and activity.
- Customers: customer records and profiles.
- Orders: all customer orders and order progress.
- Catalog: items/designs customers can browse.
- Inventory: your materials and stock quantities.
- Measurements: body measurements for customers.
- Staff: invite and manage team members.
- Settings: your shop profile, contact details, location, and images.
- Voice Assistant: hands-free actions by voice.

On mobile, use the bottom navigation bar to move between sections quickly.

## 3. First-Time Setup (Shop Owner)

### Step 1: Create your shop profile
Go to Shop Setup and fill in:
- Shop name
- Business email
- Phone (optional)
- Country, state, city
- Street address
- Shop description

### Step 2: Add your branding
Upload:
- Shop logo
- Banner image

These help customers recognize your brand in marketplace views.

### Step 3: Confirm and launch
Review your details and complete setup.
After this, you are ready to operate fully.

## 4. Customer Management (Add, Edit, Delete)

### Why this matters
Customer records are the foundation for measurements, orders, and communication.

### Add a customer
1. Open Customers.
2. Click Add Customer.
3. Fill required details (first name and last name).
4. Add optional contact/address/notes.
5. Save.

### Edit a customer
1. In Customers, find the customer.
2. Click Edit.
3. Update details.
4. Save changes.

### View customer details
1. Click View Details.
2. Review contact info and notes.
3. From this screen, you can continue to measurements when needed.

### Delete a customer
1. Click Delete.
2. Confirm the action.

Use delete only when necessary. If you may need this person again, keep the record and update notes instead.

## 5. Measurement Management (Add, Update, Delete)

### Why this matters
Accurate measurements reduce fitting problems and improve delivery quality.

### Add measurements
1. Open Measurements.
2. Click Record Measurement.
3. Select the customer.
4. Choose standard measurement fields (for example chest, waist, sleeve).
5. Enter values.
6. Add custom measurement fields if needed.
7. Add notes (optional).
8. Save.

### Update measurements
1. In the Measurements list, click Edit.
2. Update values, status, or notes.
3. Save.

### View measurements
1. Click View Details to see the full measurement record.

### Delete measurements
1. Click Delete.
2. Confirm.

Tip: keep one clean, updated record per customer so your team does not use old values by mistake.

## 6. Order Management (Create, Track, Update, Delete)

### Why this matters
Orders are where customer needs, measurements, deadlines, and payment information come together.

### Create a new order
1. Open Orders.
2. Click New Order.
3. Select customer.
4. Enter design description.
5. Add delivery date (optional but recommended).
6. Add price and notes (optional).
7. Save.

### Update an order
1. Find the order.
2. Click Edit.
3. Update status and details.
4. Save.

### Typical status flow
- Pending
- In Progress
- Completed
- Delivered
- Cancelled (if needed)

### View order details
Use View Details to review full information quickly before speaking with customer or staff.

### Delete an order
1. Click Delete.
2. Confirm.

Only delete if this order should be permanently removed from daily operations.

### Catalog order requests
Inside Orders, you can also see incoming requests submitted by marketplace visitors from catalog items.

## 7. Catalog Management (Add, Edit, Activate/Deactivate, Delete)

### Why this matters
Your catalog is what customers see when browsing your shop profile.

### Add catalog item
1. Open Catalog.
2. Click Add Item.
3. Enter item name and price.
4. Add description and image.
5. Save.

### Edit catalog item
1. Click Edit on an item.
2. Update name, price, description, or image URL.
3. Save.

### Activate or deactivate an item
- Active: visible and available.
- Inactive: hidden from active customer selection.

Use deactivate when item is temporarily unavailable.

### Delete catalog item
1. Click Delete.
2. Confirm.

If you may reuse it later, deactivate instead of deleting.

## 8. Inventory Management (Stock Control)

### Why this matters
Inventory helps you prevent stockouts and avoid overbuying.

### Add inventory item
1. Open Inventory.
2. Click Add Inventory Item.
3. Enter name (required).
4. Add SKU, quantity on hand, reorder level, unit, and pricing fields.
5. Save.

### Edit inventory item
1. Click Edit.
2. Update quantity, reorder level, and other details.
3. Save.

### Activate/deactivate item
Deactivate items that are no longer part of active stock tracking.

### Delete item
Use Delete only if the item should be removed completely.

### Low stock habit
Review low-stock items regularly and restock before urgent orders are blocked.

## 9. Staff Management (Invite, Permission Control, Remove)

### Why this matters
Staff permissions let each team member access only what they should handle.

### Invite staff member
1. Open Staff.
2. Click Invite.
3. Enter staff email.
4. Choose delivery mode:
- Email invite sent automatically, or
- Manual invite link/code sharing.
5. Send invite.

### Staff acceptance flow (what staff does)
1. Staff signs up.
2. Staff chooses Staff role.
3. Staff opens onboarding.
4. Staff accepts invite using code/link.

### Set staff permissions
For each staff member, open Permissions and choose access areas:
- Customers
- Orders
- Measurements
- Catalog
- Inventory

Save after changes.

### Remove staff access
Use Remove Staff when someone should no longer work in the shop.

### Delete staff record permanently
Use permanent delete only when you are sure the record is no longer needed.

### Resend or refresh invitation
If invite expires or is lost, use invitation refresh/resend options.

## 10. Shop Settings (Business Identity and Contact Updates)
Use Settings to keep your public and operational details correct:
- Shop name, email, phone
- Country/state/city and address
- Map location
- Logo and banner
- Shop story/description

Keep this updated so customers can contact and find you easily.

## 11. Voice Assistant (Hands-Free Work)
You can use voice to speed up repetitive tasks.

### Where to use it
- Full Voice Assistant page, and
- Floating voice assistant widget in the shop layout.

### Common actions
- Add customer
- Add measurement
- Create order
- Update order status
- List customers/orders
- Shop summary

### Best usage tip
Speak clearly, pause after your request, and confirm details before final save.

## 12. Suggested Daily Workflow

### Morning (10-15 minutes)
1. Open Dashboard: check active orders and workload.
2. Open Inventory: review low stock.
3. Open Orders: prioritize due/urgent work.

### During the day
1. Add new walk-in/online customers.
2. Record measurements immediately after fitting.
3. Create/update orders in real time.
4. Update statuses as work progresses.

### End of day
1. Confirm all completed jobs are marked correctly.
2. Check pending catalog requests.
3. Review staff access issues or missing permissions.

## 13. Suggested Weekly Workflow
1. Clean customer records (fix typos, missing phone/email).
2. Review outdated or inactive catalog items.
3. Reconcile inventory quantity with physical stock.
4. Review team permissions and remove unneeded access.
5. Update shop banner/description if promotions changed.

## 14. Common Situations and What to Do

### "A staff member cannot open Customers/Orders/etc."
- Check staff permissions in Staff section.
- Enable needed access and save.

### "Customer cannot see or request a catalog item"
- Make sure item is Active in Catalog.

### "Measurements feel outdated"
- Open customer measurement record and update values immediately.

### "Order feels stuck"
- Update status and notes so everyone knows next action.

### "Invite not received"
- Resend invite or share manual link/code.

## 15. Practical Best Practices
- Do not leave required fields empty when creating records.
- Update order status the same day work changes.
- Record measurements once and maintain them carefully.
- Use notes for special customer preferences.
- Prefer deactivation over deletion when you may need records later.
- Keep staff permissions minimal and role-based.

## 16. Quick Checklist (Owner)
- Shop profile complete
- Team invited and permissions set
- Customers updated
- Measurements recorded
- Orders current and status-tracked
- Catalog active and accurate
- Inventory monitored
- Daily review completed
